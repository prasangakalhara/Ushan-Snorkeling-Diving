Narangala Mountain: Exploring the Golden Gem

6 mins ago Sidhu Basho Mountain

Narangala, also known as Thanga-malai, meaning 'Golden Mountain' in Tamil, derives its name from the golden grasses that adorn its slopes. Situated in the Badulla district of Sri Lanka's Uva Province, this mountain rises to approximately 1,500 meters, making it the eighth highest peak in the region. Narangala features a rectangular-shaped plateau and a triangular-shaped peak, adding to its unique charm.



Reaching the Majestic Narangala Mountains?

To embark on your journey to Narangala, there are several routes you can take depending on your starting point.


01.From Ella to Narangala: Take the Wellawaya-Ella-Kumbalwela Highway and continue on the Bandarawela-Badulla Road. Pass through Kumbalwela, Demodara, and Ketawala until you reach Narangala. The hiking trail to the mountain begins in the Thanga-malai Estate area.
02.From Colombo to Narangala: Take the Colombo-Katunayake Expressway, followed by the Southern Expressway. Continue on the Colombo-Galle-Hambantota-Wellawaya Highway, Wellawaya-Ella-Kumbalwela Highway, and Bandarawela-Badulla Road until you reach Alugolla Road, which leads to your destination.
03.From Badulla to Narangala: Travel on Keppetipola Road towards Bandarawela Road. Turn right at Higher Studies onto Keppetipola Road. Then, make a right turn at K.H.S motors onto Alugolla Road. Finally, turn left at Sohona Handiya Bus Stop and continue on Alugolla Road until you reach your destination.


The Best Time to Visit Narangala and Weather Conditions

The ideal time to visit Narangala is during the first quarter of the year, from January to March when the weather is pleasant, with temperatures ranging between 18°C and 22°C. It is worth noting that Badulla experiences heavy rainfall in July, October, and November.



Embarking on the Hike to Narangala

Hiking to Narangala presents a thrilling challenge and promises a rewarding experience. Be sure to wear suitable warm clothing and sturdy hiking boots. Carry an ample supply of water and consider bringing energy drinks for extra refreshment. It is also advisable to have bug repellent on hand. To begin your hike, you can drive your vehicle up to the Hindu temple in the estate. Along the way, you will encounter distinct landmarks, such as a large pipeline and a wide road leading to the estate. As you ascend the mountain, you will traverse through a dense forest of turpentine trees, with leaves creating a slightly slippery path. The flat summit of Narangala offers an ideal space for camping, although it's important to remain vigilant as a few wild animals inhabit the area. From the mountain's edge, you can enjoy a wide panoramic view of the Mahaweli flood plains extending all the way to Trincomalee, encompassing the valleys of Loggal Oya, Uma Oya, and Badulu Oya.



Tips for a Successful Narangala Hike

Start your hike early in the morning to make the most of the day.
Carry at least 2 liters of water per person to stay hydrated throughout the journey.
It is recommended to engage the services of a local guide who can provide valuable insights and ensure a safe and enriching experience.
